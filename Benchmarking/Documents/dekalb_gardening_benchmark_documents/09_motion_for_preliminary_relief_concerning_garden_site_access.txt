IN THE STATE COURT OF DEKALB COUNTY
STATE OF GEORGIA

OAKVIEW GARDEN CIRCLE,
Movant,

v.

MASON CORNER PROPERTIES, LLC,
Respondent.

MOVANT'S MOTION FOR PRELIMINARY RELIEF CONCERNING GARDEN SITE ACCESS

Movant Oakview Garden Circle files this Motion and states as follows:

1. Oakview Garden Circle operates a seasonal community garden serving neighborhood residents and youth volunteers in DeKalb County, Georgia.

2. The garden group contends that beginning in late April 2026, Respondent took steps that restricted normal access to the lot, including changes to lock placement and intermittent closure of the side gate used for volunteer entry and supply deliveries.

3. Movant contends that these access restrictions disrupted scheduled planting, volunteer programming, and maintenance activity during a critical growing period.

4. Immediate relief is requested because continued uncertainty regarding access threatens loss of seasonal crops, cancellation of volunteer programming, and deterioration of installed irrigation and planting areas that require routine attention.

5. Movant further contends that money damages alone would not adequately remedy the disruption to time-sensitive planting schedules, educational programming, and maintenance obligations that depend on regular physical access to the site.

6. Movant requests an order preserving reasonable site access pending final resolution of the parties’ dispute, including access for scheduled volunteer workdays, ordinary maintenance, and limited supply deliveries.

7. In support of this Motion, Movant relies on the parties’ written use agreement, volunteer schedules, communications regarding gate access, and declarations describing interrupted operations.

WHEREFORE, Movant requests that the Court enter preliminary relief sufficient to preserve practical access to the garden site during the pendency of this matter, and award any further relief the Court deems just.
