DEKALB COMMUNITY GARDEN SAFETY AND VOLUNTEER PROCEDURES MANUAL
Excerpted Operating Rules

1. Purpose
These procedures establish minimum operating rules for volunteer-managed community garden sites supported by the DeKalb Community Garden Partnership.

2. Incident Reporting
Any injury, unsafe condition, damaged equipment, unauthorized entry, or significant conflict occurring at a garden site shall be reported to the site coordinator as soon as practicable. A written incident entry shall be made in the site log no later than the end of the same day if a coordinator is present, or by noon of the next day if the issue is first identified after a workday ends.

3. Tool Storage
Tools shall be returned to the designated shed, locker, or secured storage area at the end of each work period. Sharp tools shall not be left unattended in common walkways. Access codes or keys shall be limited to coordinators and specifically authorized lead volunteers.

4. Chemical and Treatment Product Storage
Fertilizers, treatment products, and similar materials shall be kept in labeled containers and stored only in designated areas not accessible to general volunteers unless supervised by an authorized person.

5. Volunteer Supervision
General volunteers shall work under the direction of a site coordinator or an authorized lead volunteer. Sites shall not be opened for general work activity unless at least one authorized supervisor is present.

6. Site Cleanliness and Closing Procedures
At the end of each work period, volunteers shall remove trash, stack unused materials neatly, secure storage areas, and confirm that gates are closed.

7. Record Retention
Incident logs and related site safety records shall be retained for at least one year from the date of entry.

8. Reporting Escalation
If an unsafe condition presents an immediate risk of injury or substantial property damage, the site coordinator shall notify the program director promptly and may suspend volunteer activity until the condition is addressed.
