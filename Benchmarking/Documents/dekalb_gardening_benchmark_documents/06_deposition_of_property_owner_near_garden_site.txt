EXCERPT OF DEPOSITION OF DANIEL PRICE
Owner of Adjacent Property

Taken on May 10, 2026

Q. Please state your name.
A. Daniel Price.

Q. Where do you reside?
A. I own the property adjacent to the lot used for the community garden on Mason Avenue in DeKalb County.

Q. Did you make complaints about water runoff from the garden site?
A. Yes. I complained more than once in March and again in April because after heavy rain the water came toward the low point near my side yard.

Q. Do you remember when you first raised that issue?
A. The first time I remember clearly was March 12, 2026. I emailed the garden group contact listed on a sign near the fence.

Q. Did you later speak with anyone in person?
A. Yes. I spoke with a woman named Carla during a Saturday workday later in March, maybe around the twenty-second or twenty-third.

Q. What did you tell her?
A. I said the runoff seemed worse after they reshaped one of the planting areas and stacked soil bags near the fence line.

Q. Did you observe actual damage?
A. I observed muddy water crossing into my yard and pooling near a corner of my fence. I also believed the fence posts were becoming unstable, though I am not an engineer.

Q. Were complaints made about fencing as well?
A. Yes. I also complained that a new section of temporary fencing was placed close to the property line and I did not think it had been discussed with me first.

Q. Did anyone give you notice before the fence installation?
A. I was not personally given advance notice before the temporary fence section went up. After it was installed, someone from the garden group told me it was intended to keep compost bins away from the walking path.

Q. Do you know who told you that?
A. I believe it was a volunteer coordinator, but I do not remember the name.

Q. Did you ever receive written notice afterward?
A. I received an email in early April explaining that the fence placement was temporary and would be reviewed.

Q. Did you contact the property owner?
A. I contacted the property management company by phone after I felt the garden group was not moving quickly enough.

Q. Did you take photographs?
A. Yes. I took photographs after a rain event in April showing pooling water and the area near the fence.

Q. Did you ever tell anyone the runoff had stopped?
A. I said it looked better after one of the trenches was cleared, but I still had concerns after later storms.
