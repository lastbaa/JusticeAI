EXCERPT OF DEPOSITION OF MAYA THOMPSON
Garden Program Coordinator for Eastside Growers Collective

Taken on May 8, 2026

Q. Please state your name and role for the record.
A. My name is Maya Thompson. I am the garden program coordinator for Eastside Growers Collective, and I oversee volunteers, scheduling, and storage access for the Glenwood Road garden.

Q. Did you have responsibility for the tool shed at that location?
A. Yes. I handled the lock code distribution and made sure tools were put back after volunteer workdays.

Q. Who had access to the locked tool shed in March and April of 2026?
A. I had access. Our assistant coordinator, Luis Ramirez, had access. We also gave the lock code to two lead volunteers, Anita Brooks and Jerome Lee, because they opened the site on Saturdays.

Q. Was the code given to all volunteers?
A. No. Regular volunteers did not receive the code. They could only enter the shed when one of the authorized people was present.

Q. Did that policy change at any point?
A. Not really. We did remind people more often in April because tools had been left out twice after weekend cleanup.

Q. When were volunteers first warned about restricted storage areas?
A. I first mentioned it at the March 23 volunteer orientation. I explained that the shed and the small supply cage near the rear fence were restricted. Then I repeated the warning in a follow-up email before the April 5 workday.

Q. Do you recall why you sent the April 5 email?
A. Yes. Someone had stored loose bags of fertilizer in the wrong area, and I wanted to remind everyone that supplies were only to be handled by authorized volunteers or staff.

Q. Were there occasions when volunteers were on-site without staff?
A. Occasionally there were Saturday mornings when a lead volunteer opened the gate before I arrived, but one of the authorized people was there. We did not permit general volunteer access without supervision.

Q. Were any complaints made about the way tools were stored?
A. I heard concerns that a wheelbarrow and several rakes were left near the side gate in late March, but those were moved the same day once I was informed.

Q. Did you ever tell volunteers that the storage restrictions were optional?
A. No, never.

Q. Did you supervise volunteer activity directly?
A. On most scheduled workdays, yes. If I could not attend, Luis or one of the approved lead volunteers supervised.

Q. Did you keep written records of incidents?
A. We kept an incident log for injuries, unsafe conditions, or damaged equipment. Routine reminders were usually handled by email.

Q. Was there any issue with site access in early April?
A. The front gate latch was sticking for a few days, but the site remained accessible. We asked everyone to use the side entrance temporarily.
