LANDSCAPE AND IRRIGATION CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT

This Landscape and Irrigation Contractor Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into on April 3, 2026, by and between DeKalb Youth Garden Lab (“Owner”) and Green Row Installations, Inc. (“Contractor”).

1. Project
Owner engages Contractor to install and test a drip irrigation system, hose bib connection, and related water distribution components at the teaching garden located at 922 Columbia Drive, DeKalb County, Georgia.

2. Contractor Responsibilities
Contractor shall:
(a) furnish labor and ordinary installation tools;
(b) install the irrigation lines in accordance with the site plan provided by Owner;
(c) connect the system to the designated water source;
(d) test zones for visible leaks and flow continuity;
(e) remove installation debris and leave work areas in reasonably clean condition; and
(f) complete final walkthrough with Owner’s site representative.

3. Schedule
Work shall commence no later than April 15, 2026, and substantial completion shall occur by April 25, 2026, subject to weather delays or Owner-caused access delays. Contractor shall notify Owner promptly if any condition is likely to delay completion by more than two business days.

4. Compensation
Owner shall pay Contractor a total contract sum of $4,200, payable as follows: forty percent (40%) upon mobilization, forty percent (40%) upon installation of the main system components, and twenty percent (20%) upon substantial completion and walkthrough.

5. Insurance
Contractor shall maintain commercial general liability insurance in commercially reasonable amounts for a contractor performing on a small public-facing site and shall maintain workers’ compensation coverage if required by law. Upon request, Contractor shall provide proof of coverage to Owner before beginning work.

6. Safety and Site Protection
Contractor shall exercise reasonable care to avoid damage to raised beds, fencing, and instructional materials stored on-site. Contractor shall keep trenches, hoses, and tools in a condition that does not create unnecessary tripping hazards during active work.

7. Indemnification
To the fullest extent permitted by law, Contractor shall indemnify and hold harmless Owner, its officers, and volunteers from claims, damages, or losses arising out of Contractor’s negligent acts or omissions, or those of persons acting under Contractor’s control, in connection with the work. Contractor shall not be responsible for preexisting site conditions not caused by Contractor.

8. Independent Contractor
Contractor is an independent contractor and is solely responsible for its employees, taxes, and means of performing the work.

9. Termination
Owner may terminate this Agreement for material breach if Contractor fails to cure such breach within five (5) business days after written notice. If terminated without Contractor fault, Contractor shall be paid for work properly performed through the date of termination.

10. Governing Law
Georgia law shall govern this Agreement.
